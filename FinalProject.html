    Dale Carnegie (1888-1955)

    Dale Carnegie (/ˈkɑːrnɪɡi/ KAR-nig-ee;[1] spelled Carnagey until c. 1922; November 24, 1888 – November 1, 1955) was an American writer and lecturer, and the developer of courses in self-improvement, salesmanship, corporate training, public speaking, and interpersonal skills.

  2. Works:

    • The art of Public speaking (1956) - A Practical Guide to the Art of Public Speaking.

    • How To Stop Worrying and Start Living (1948) - Tips for overcoming worry and living a fulfilling life.

    • How to Win Friends and Influence People (1936) - A classic about human relationships and social influence.
